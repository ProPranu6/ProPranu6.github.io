About Me

I am an incoming Machine Learning Engineer Intern at Modlee Inc., a fast-growing startup, where I will focus on backend development and building cutting-edge large language model (LLM) and deep learning (DL) projects.

Currently, I am a Research Assistant at the Big Data in Biotransport Center at the University of Southern California, where I work on ocular vasculature analysis. My research involves leveraging advanced deep learning models such as U-Net, Attention U-Net, and Vision Transformer (ViT) architectures for microvasculature segmentation and biomarker extraction. This work is directed toward identifying cardiovascular disease (CVD) biomarkers and contributing to the development of novel diagnostic tools.

Previously, I worked as a Graduate Student Researcher at the Center for Artificial Intelligence in Society (CAIS) at USC. My work at CAIS focused on constructing preprocessing pipelines for parsing, structuring, and aggregating passive digital phenotypes from wearable devices. I researched and identified predictive features for Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) events using time series analysis and machine learning techniques.

For the upcoming semester, I will be serving as a grader for the Foundations of Data Management course (DSCI 551) at USC, assisting in evaluating coursework and providing feedback to students on core data management concepts.

Before joining USC, I was a Deep Learning Researcher at the International Center for Neuromorphic Systems at Western Sydney University, where I developed a deep learning-based EEG classification strategy inspired by EEGNet. This work achieved a per-sample classification accuracy of 83%, demonstrating its effectiveness across multiple subjects in brain-computer interface applications.

I am passionate about applying artificial intelligence to solve critical challenges in healthcare and for social good. My work has been recognized with multiple honors, including a Gold Medal and Jury Awards for Impactful AI/ML Research Presentations on University Research Day 2021 & 2022 and a selection for the Amazon ML Summer School 2022. Additionally, I have contributed to the research community with publications in reputed journals such as IEEE Transactions on Network and Service Management and remain committed to advancing AI research and technology.
